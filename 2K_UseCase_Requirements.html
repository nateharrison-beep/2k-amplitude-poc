Context

Why this document exists

Through four months of discovery, POC cadence calls, and one-on-one conversations with engineering, BI, product, and telemetry leadership at 2K, a clear picture has emerged: 2K needs more than campaign delivery. They need a unified player intelligence layer that standardizes how all 150+ data team members measure players across titles, supports rich journey analytics for game design decisions, and progressively automates insight discovery — areas where CleverTap has no answer.

📊

5–6 Year Standardization Initiative

David Dillane & Saurabh Singh (BI team) are mid-way through a company-wide effort to standardize player metrics and journey reporting across all 150 data analysts and every game title portfolio. Amplitude has the opportunity to attach to this initiative as a self-service reporting interface that enhances and accelerates it — not compete with the underlying data work already in flight.

🗺️

Journey Analytics Gap

Current tools (Tableau, Looker) provide rigid, pre-built reports with no self-serve pathway analysis. Game designers and product leads can't ask free-form questions about how players move through the game without SQL or engineering support. Marketers who understand the complete journey — what a player did before receiving an offer, what they did after, and where they dropped off — can optimize far more precisely than those working from isolated campaign metrics alone.

🤖

Agentic Intelligence Vision

Kit, Tariq, Ed, and Jennifer all independently surfaced interest in proactive monitoring, anomaly detection, and AI-powered reporting. 2K is building an internal AI product for marketing teams — Amplitude's MCP integration positions it as the behavioral data layer for that system.

Strategic Framing

Why Amplitude wins — and why CleverTap can't

CleverTap operates at the last mile: it delivers messages to players and tracks campaign engagement. But everything that happens before a message is sent — understanding player behavior, building intelligent audiences, governing the data, and proactively detecting what's going wrong — requires a platform like Amplitude.

Amplitude vs. CleverTap: The Strategic Distinction

Amplitude

Player intelligence platform — understands what players do, why they do it, what will happen next, and how to measure it consistently across every title. Proactive, agentic, self-serve. Amplitude doesn't replace 2K's existing Snowflake, Databricks, and Kafka investments — it makes them smarter.

CleverTap

Last-mile delivery platform — sends campaigns to audiences that someone else built. Reactive by design. No journey analytics, no schema governance, no AI reasoning layer. Cannot enrich the work 2K's data team has already done.

The AI & MCP Advantage

Amplitude's MCP integration connects directly into 2K's existing AI initiatives (Cortex, Databricks Genie) — adding proactive anomaly detection, natural language querying, and always-on monitoring on top of the behavioral data layer they've already built. Qualitative signals from Steam reviews and social sentiment feed directly into this loop.

Why Mixpanel also falls short

No agentic AI, no MCP integration, no schema governance at 2K's scale, and no qualitative sentiment layer. 2K currently uses Mixpanel for mobile titles and views it as "less sophisticated." It can report metrics but can't connect them to player sentiment, power an AI agent, or govern schema across a multi-title portfolio.
